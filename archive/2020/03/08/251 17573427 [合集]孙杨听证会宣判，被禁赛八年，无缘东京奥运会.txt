北京时间2月28日17时，国际体育仲裁法庭（CAS）公布了“世界反兴奋剂机构（WADA）诉孙杨和国际泳联案听证会”的裁决书，孙杨被禁赛八年，即日起生效。
　　现场，秘书长在国际体育仲裁法庭总部向媒体发表简短声明，并亲自宣布裁决结果。
　　此次裁决结果出炉之后并不是最终的结果，如果孙杨胜诉则证明清白，若败诉的话，孙杨可以上诉至瑞士联邦法院。
　　听证会起因于2018年9月4日，孙杨在杭州接受了一次赛外兴奋剂检查，由于对检查人员出示的资质证明存疑，此次检查最终未完成。2019年1月，国际泳联裁决此次检查无效，孙杨不存在违反《世界反兴奋剂条例》的行为。两个月后，WADA因不满裁决结果，向国际体育仲裁法庭提出上诉。
　　2019年11月15日，孙杨一案听证会瑞士蒙特勒公开举行，时间长达十小时，还通过网络进行了全球直播。听证会上，孙杨及其律师团队拿出了大量证据，试图还原事件真相，捍卫自己的清白和合法权益。
　　孙杨方面指出，当天检查人员的多项涉嫌违规做法，如检查人员无法出具相关资质证明、尿检人员对孙杨进行拍照从而违反兴奋剂检查规定等。参与兴奋剂检查的三名检查人员并没有出席当天的听证会。
　　孙杨现年28岁，是中国游泳队队长，2012年伦敦奥运会他成为中国男子游泳首位奥运冠军。孙杨在奥运会共获得3金2银1铜，从2009-2019年游泳世锦赛，孙杨共获得11金2银3铜。

贴一个 澳洲记者的英文报道
er Sun Yang banned for eight years
By Phil Lutton
February 28, 2020 — 8.12pm
Sun Yang's swimming career is over, with the Court of Arbitration for Sport banning the Chinese star for eight years for his role in tampering with a doping sample.
CAS upheld the appeal by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) after a FINA panel originally let Sun continue to compete after a vial of blood was smashed during an out-of-competition test in September of 2018.
It's a bombshell result that ends a tumultuous career that saw him win three Olympic gold medals and sparked a bitter rivalry with Australia's Mack Horton. Sun is China's most popular athlete and the reaction will be substantial.
Chinese swimmer Sun Yang.
Chinese swimmer Sun Yang.Credit:AP
In the ruling, CAS said Sun tampered with the sample, which carries an eight-year penalty, and he "failed to establish that he had a compelling justification to destroy the sample collection containers". But given subsequent tests were negative and FINA did not give him a provisional ban, his results until Friday's finding will all stand.
The news will rock swimming just five months out from the Tokyo Olympic Games, where Sun had hoped to add to his three gold medals collected in London and Rio.
WADA released a statement saying they welcomed the ruling and that they were now satisfied 'justice had been rendered'.
The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) welcomes the ruling of the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) in relation to WADA’s appeal against the Fédération Internationale de Natation (FINA) disciplinary panel decision in relation to an incident that led to a doping control involving Chinese swimmer Sun Yang not being completed as planned," WADA said.
"WADA had lodged the appeal on the basis that Sun Yang voluntarily refused to submit to sample collection as per the terms of the World Anti-Doping Code (Code) and the related International Standard for Testing and Investigations. WADA notes the sanction handed down by CAS and is satisfied that justice in this case has been rendered."
Horton has refused to buy into the matter but in a statement said: "My stance has always been for clean sport. It is not, and never will be about individuals or nations. Today’s outcome does not change my stance."
Earlier on Friday, Australia's head swimming coach Jacco Verhaeren said Horton would not be concerned whether Sun swam in Tokyo or not. But his ban is validation for the hardline he took in Rio, when he called Sun a 'drug cheat', and when he refused to share a podium with him at last year's World Championships.
The ban is a fullstop on a career that mixed brilliance and was never short of drama. He was an undoubted champion in the pool but the stench of anti-doping concerns were never far away. They went to a new level when Horton called him out, then beat him in the 400m final, in Rio.
Sun was first seen at the Olympics in Beijing in 2008, when he made the final of the 1500m freestyle when he was just 16. His rise from there was rapid; by 2011 he was a world champion in the 800m and the world champion and world record holder in the 1500m.
He won gold in the 400m and 1500m at the London Olympics and silver in the 200m, cementing his reputation as one of the greatest freestyler swimmers in the sport. A year later, he completed the 400-800-1500m trifecta at the World Championships.
By this point Sun was a megastar in China but controversy was rarely far away. In 2014 he tested positive for trimetazidine, a stimulant he said he was taking for a heart condition that had only recently been added to the WADA list of prohibited substances.
Instead of a two-year ban, Sun was banned by China's anti-doping agency for just three months, a ban which was only announced after it had been served and just before another golden run at the 2014 Asian Games and Chinese national championships.
That heightened suspicion among his fellow swimmers, while his mannerisms in and around the pool deck made him less than popular with rivals. It didn't have any impact on his swimming; he collected double gold at the 2015 World Championships.
Sun's real notoriety in Australia came from his spectacular showdown with Horton at the Rio Games. Before the finals of the 400m, Horton called Sun a 'drug cheat' then went on to win gold, citing it as a win for the 'good guys'.
Horton was swamped by abuse from Sun's millions of Chinese fans but refused to back down. It would be just the start of a simmering fued that would go to even great levels three years later in the wake of Sun's dramatic incident with drug testers in September of 2018.
After questioning the validity of the the officials charged with taking blood and urine samples during an out-of-competition test at his home, Sun's mother was alleged to have ordered security guards to destroy a vial of Sun's blood with a hammer.
No sample was taken for testing and a FINA doping panel would controversially let Sun off with a warning and not a ban. Soon after the matter was made public in the media, WADA appealed the matter to the Court of Arbitration for Sport and have now got their man.
That matter was still waiting to be heard when Sun appeared at the FINA World Championships in South Korea in 2019. It created a storm of protest among vocal swimmers and Horton took the lead once again, refusing to share a podium with Sun after the 400m freestyle final.
Scotsman Duncan Scott was another that wanted nothing to do with Sun on the podium, refusing to shake hands and pose for photographs after the 200m freestyle final. It was a tense, incendiary meet that set the scene for Sun's public hearing in front of the CAS panel late last year.
That would now seem to be the final act for Sun, while Horton will rise again to focus on defending his title in Tokyo.
